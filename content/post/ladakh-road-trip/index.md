There's a particular kind of silence you only find above 4000 metres. The air is thin,
the sky is impossibly blue, and the mountains stretch in every direction like they're
trying to make you feel small on purpose. Ladakh does this well.

## The Route

We started from Manali, taking the classic loop:

```
Manali → Keylong → Sarchu → Leh
Leh → Khardung La → Nubra Valley → Pangong Lake
Pangong → Tso Moriri → Manali
```

Total: ~1500 km across 10 days. Two riders, two motorcycles, one tent, and an optimistic
medical kit.

## Day 1–2: Manali to Leh via Sarchu

The Manali–Leh highway is spectacular and exhausting in equal measure.
The road climbs through five high passes — Rohtang, Baralacha La, Lachulung La,
Nakee La, and Tanglang La (5328 m, the second-highest motorable pass in the world).

**Sarchu** sits at 4253 m and serves as the midpoint camp. We stayed in a tent hotel —
basic but warm. The night sky here is one of the clearest I've seen anywhere;
no light pollution, the Milky Way visible with the naked eye.

> Altitude tip: don't rush. Acclimatise in Manali for at least a day before heading up.
> Diamox (acetazolamide) is worth having, but drink water religiously.

## Day 3–4: Leh

Leh is the kind of place that earns its reputation. The old town, Leh Palace perched on
the hillside, and the market lane are all worth a slow morning. We spent two days here
to acclimatise properly before pushing higher.

Recommended: **Shanti Stupa** at sunset. Get there early — the crowd fills up fast.

## Day 5–6: Nubra Valley via Khardung La

**Khardung La** at 5359 m is the headline — often cited as the world's highest motorable
road (disputed, but the views are indisputable). The descent into Nubra is dramatic:
the valley floor is green, there are sand dunes at **Hunder**, and Bactrian camels wander
past like they own the place. Because they do.

## Day 7–8: Pangong Lake

The road from Nubra to Pangong via Shyok valley is the most technically demanding
section — loose gravel, river crossings, no guardrails. Worth every minute.

**Pangong Tso** is 134 km long, stretches into China, and changes colour through the day
from turquoise to cobalt to deep blue. Photographs don't capture it. You need to sit
beside it for an hour.

## Day 9–10: Tso Moriri and back to Manali

Tso Moriri is less visited than Pangong but arguably more beautiful. We had the lake
almost to ourselves at sunrise. The return to Manali via the Leh–Manali highway felt
faster somehow — familiarity compresses distance.

## Practical Notes

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Best season | June – September |
| Permits | ILP required for Nubra, Pangong, Tso Moriri |
| Fuel | Fill up in Leh — sparse stations beyond |
| Connectivity | Near zero outside Leh; download offline maps |
| Budget | ₹3000–5000/day (fuel, food, stay) |
